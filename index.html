Saving Lives

Simple illnesses and malnutrition should never threaten lives. Our feeding programs and medical clinics work together to rescue children from severe malnutrition.

Childhood death from malnutrition is a chronic problem in East Africa, but preventable through early discovery and immediate intervention through food and medical treatment. Last year we fed over 128,000 people, including 20,000 children who would likely have died otherwise.

We've provided medical care to almost 400,000 people in East Africa since 2006, and we have no intention to stop. In December 2016, we opened the first pediatric medical clinic in a region of East Africa. With your help, we can continue saving lives.

Changing Futures

At Mercy Chapel, we rescue victims of sex trafficking, house and care for them, and train them in vocational skills for a new life.

Some girls in East Africa never have the chance to grow up in a safe environment: predators catch them and enslave them with debt in the red light district. Mercy Chapel was once a brothel. Now it is a beacon of hope and vocational training center for girls still trapped in the red light district. They learn skills like hair dressing, computers, and leatherworking. Upon graduation we work with the girls to help them find new jobs. The girls also receive counseling and spiritual training. More than 90% of the girls graduating from Mercy Chapel receive Jesus and their Lord and Savior during their time at the Mercy Chapel.

The overwhelming response to this program testifies to the great need. We have graduated over 300 girls, and with your help, we can help many more.
